Hydra

When a Hydra loses one head, its body actually allows it to grow two more in its place. This gives it a strong survival advantage in the wild.

  • Scientific Name: Tyrannosaurus Hydras
  • Average Length: 400-500 meters
  • Average Lifespan: Immortal
  • Habitat: Ocean Depths/Hades

One of the oldest reptillians to walk the Earth, the Hydra is commonly known to cause terror to regional fishing expeditions, oceanic industry, and Greek heroes. Thanks to a series of genetic mutations, this creature is impervious to most mortal instruments of death. It is not currently known how many Hydra exist in the wild, but estimations lead some to believe this elusive beast is responsible for most of the predation in the world's oceans.
